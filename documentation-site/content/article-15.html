Training A Custom Model with Google Cloud Vision API

Step 1: Introduction and Problem Statement

I have a garage dooor that seems to open itself. Either someone keeps leaving it open, or it has developed sentience and is opening itself. I doubt the second ooption, but now I need to determine when my garage dooor is open so I can close it.

This scene does not lend itself well to using our existing analytics to determine if the door is open or closed. I also do not want to wire a traditional contact sensor to the garage door. This means I will need to train a custom machine learning model to tell if the dooor has been left open.

Step 2: Gathering Images for Training

I can use the preview images from the Eagle Eye API to give me plenty of training data. I have known times when the door is open and closed. The trick will be to gather enough representative examples so that changing lighting conditions do to not cause false matches.

We will start by using the included python script (EE-training/preview_downloader.py) to download images into two different folders (open and closed). We will be manually selecting times where the door is in the disered position and there is not motion in the scene that might confuse the model.
